📖 Biography

This is Mariam Mustazaf, an Afghan youth passionate about Medical Data Science, Computer Science, and promoting women's rights in Afghanistan.

Born in Afghanistan — a country of rich culture and history — I grew up amid the contrasting beauty of ancient traditions and modern challenges. From an early age, I have been driven to learn and discover knowledge through science and mathematics; making me a strong candidate to receive a scholarship at Afghan-Turk Maarif International Girls High School in Kabul, where I successfully completed my contemporary education among thousands of girls applying for the program in 2019.

"Every catastrophe is also an opportunity." This quote perfectly encapsulates my journey and my passion for perseverance. When schools got banned for girls beyond grade six in 2021, I chose to focus on the bright side of this tragic situation.

Therefore, I became one of the top 10 nominees for the 2021 Children’s Peace Prize among candidates from around the world for the recognition of my voluntary efforts to educate and mentor Afghan women and children. Over seven years of my volunteer jobs (2019-2026), I provided educational support, mentoring, and tutoring to over 1000 women at the Khadija Kubra Islamic Educational Center, the Lincoln Learning Center, and via home-schooling programs.

Currently, I am pursuing my Bachelor's degree in Computer Science at an American university, where I am expanding my knowledge in programming, data structures, algorithms, and software development. Alongside my studies, I have worked professionally as an English-Persian Interpreter and Translator, bridging communication gaps in medical and professional settings.

🌱 Background

Throughout my academic and volunteer journey, I have worked with several national and international organizations, expanding my knowledge in order to help Afghan women and children.

In January 2024, I joined the Amala learning bridge program for raising awareness about mental illness through online platforms. As a result, we were able to help and raise awareness among 500+ Afghan women in our 3-month seminars.

Later, I co-founded the Parvaaz Women’s Mental Health Support Foundation and continued our journey to support Afghan women's mental health by providing weekly and monthly online sessions, reading book sessions, an online library, and one-on-one therapist support for over 700+ girls through our 8-month journey.

Moreover, being accepted into the Alekain Foundation became a life-changing opportunity for me, enabling me to pursue my educational journey and bringing me closer to my greatest ambition: becoming one of the first medical data scientists in Afghanistan. My experience as an interpreter and translator has strengthened my communication skills and deepened my understanding of both the medical and tech fields.

As a dedicated and goal-oriented Afghan girl who has devoted her life to empowering Afghan youths, I am determined to earn my bachelor's in Computer Science and Medical Data Science, create my own non-profit organization to support individuals from vulnerable countries who are passionate about AI and technology, and leverage its potentials for positive and ethical implementation in the world.
